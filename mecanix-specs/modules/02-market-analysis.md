# 2. Market Analysis

## 2.1 Target Markets

### Angola
- Estimated 800K+ registered vehicles, concentrated in Luanda
- High-value corporate and oil sector fleet maintenance market
- USD and AOA dual-currency environment — premium pricing possible
- Near-zero direct software competition in workshop management
- Power and connectivity constraints make offline-first critical
- Growing insurance sector with major players (ENSA, Saham, Fidelidade) underserved by digital tools
- First-mover advantage available — ideal launch market

### Mozambique
- ~400K registered vehicles; growing vehicle import market
- M-Pesa dominant payment infrastructure — integration is a key differentiator
- Insurance penetration growing but claims processes entirely paper-based
- Very low digital adoption in workshops — education required
- Smaller revenue per customer but low acquisition cost
- Similar regulatory environment to Angola — easy to extend

### Brazil
- 115M+ registered vehicles — 3rd largest auto market globally
- Mandatory NF-e (Nota Fiscal Eletronica) fiscal integration with SEFAZ
- Mature insurance market (Susep-regulated) with high digital expectations
- Existing competition (Oficina Next, AutoForce, Mecanizou) but mostly desktop-first
- Strong WhatsApp culture — customers expect digital communication
- Highest revenue potential — enter in Phase 3 after Angola/Mozambique validation

## 2.2 Go-to-Market Sequence

| Phase | Market | Timeline | Key Focus |

| Starter | 1 location, up to 3 users | $49/month | R$199/month | 500 job cards/month |
| Professional | 1 location, unlimited users | $99/month | R$399/month | Unlimited + reporting |
| Multi-Site | Chains, 2–10 locations | $249/month | R$999/month | Multi-location dashboard |
| White-Label | Dealer groups | Custom | Custom | Full branding + API access |
| Insurance | Insurance companies | $199/month | R$799/month | Assessor portal + analytics |

> **New Revenue Stream: Insurance** — The Insurance tier is a B2B2B model: workshops pay for their subscription, and insurance companies pay separately for the assessor portal. This creates a network effect — more workshops on MECANIX makes it more attractive for insurers, and vice versa. Target: 5 insurance partnerships in Angola by month 12.
